**Comprehensive Weekly Report: Gold (GC=F) Markets & Macro Trends (March 18–25, 2026)**

### 1. Executive Summary
Gold prices (GC=F) surged over the past week, driven primarily by escalating geopolitical tensions surrounding the US–Iran conflict and shifting market expectations of a potential ceasefire. The metal gained approximately **3.5–4.0%** on Wednesday alone, breaking a multi-session losing streak and pushing toward the $4,600/oz psychological level. Concurrently, crude oil prices fell on hopes of de-escalation, reinforcing gold’s traditional safe-haven appeal. Macro data showed a softer US dollar and declining Treasury yields, further bolstering gold’s upside. ETF inflows into gold products remained robust, with SPDR Gold Shares (GLD) surpassing $180 billion in AUM amid strong retail and institutional demand. Mining equities also rallied, reflecting improved sentiment toward the sector.

### 2. Geopolitical Drivers: The US–Iran Conflict
- **Ceasefire Dynamics**: Multiple reports (Bloomberg, WSJ, Reuters) indicate the US has drafted a 15‑point plan to end the war with Iran. While Iran publicly rejected the proposal and continued attacks, markets interpreted the diplomatic effort as a step toward potential de‑escalation, reducing risk‑off pressure on oil and boosting gold.
- **Market Reaction**: Gold extended gains after snapping a nine‑day losing streak; oil (CL=F, BZ=F) tumbled 4%+ on ceasefire hopes (Investor’s Business Daily, GuruFocus). Asian and European equities rose while oil fell, reflecting a risk‑on shift in equities but sustained haven demand for gold (Reuters, WSJ).
- **Strategic Shift**: TotalEnergies announced a capital reallocation from wind to natural gas, citing the “Trump Trade” and expectations of prolonged fossil‑fuel demand amid Middle East uncertainty (TipRanks). This underscores broader energy‑sector realignment that could keep oil volatility elevated, indirectly supporting gold as a hedge.

### 3. Macro & Monetary Environment
- **Dollar & Yields**: Gold rose off a near‑three‑month low as the US dollar weakened and Treasury yields eased (MT Newswires). A softer dollar reduces the opportunity cost of holding non‑yielding bullion, while lower yields diminish the attractiveness of alternative safe‑haven assets like government bonds.
- **Inflation Concerns**: Jamie Dimon warned of “stubborn inflation” exacerbated by the Iran war‑induced oil price surge, suggesting that even if oil retreats on peace hopes, structural inflationary pressures may persist (24/7 Wall St.). This reinforces gold’s long‑term appeal as an inflation hedge.
- **Liquidity & ETF Flows**: GLD surpassed $180 billion in AUM, fueled by roughly $15 billion in net inflows in Q1 2026 alone (24/7 Wall St.). This record level reflects sustained institutional and retail appetite for gold exposure amid macro uncertainty.

### 4. Gold‑Specific News & Market Structure
- **Price Action**: Gold jumped 4% to ~$4,580/oz on Wednesday morning, clawing back toward $4,600 (WSJ). intraday volatility remained elevated as traders parsed diplomatic headlines.
- **Mining Sector Strength**: Freeport‑McMoRan, Royal Gold, and Newmont rose 2.1%, 1.4%, and 3.2% respectively after WSJ reported on the US‑drafted peace plan (Barrons). Silver futures also gained 3.9%, indicating broad precious‑metals momentum.
- **Corporate Developments**: 
  - Integra Resources highlighted a “transformative year” post‑acquisition of the Florida Canyon Mine, shifting from developer to US gold producer (MarketBeat).
  - Andean Precious Metals reported a “step change” in 2025 results, driven by record financial performance and strengthened balance sheet (MarketBeat).
  - Pan Global Resources and Bravo Mining reported high‑grade gold drill results in Spain and North Sector, respectively (MT Newswires), supporting exploration‑driven upside.
- **Valuation & Technical Views**: 
  - Wedbush Fund Advisers’ Cullen Rogers noted gold ETFs are moving in tandem with energy ETFs amid geopolitical uncertainty (Yahoo Finance Video).
  - Barrons highlighted gold’s recent drop as a buying opportunity, citing oversold conditions and improving sentiment (Barrons).
  - Investor’s Business Daily suggested Agnico Eagle Mines (AEM) may stabilize after a pullback, offering options‑based entry points (IBD).

### 5. Risks & Countervailing Forces
- **Ceasefire Failure**: If Iran’s rejection leads to renewed hostilities, oil could spike again, potentially triggering a risk‑off episode that might initially drag gold down alongside other commodities (though historically, gold rebounds sharply in true crisis scenarios).
- **Dollar Rebound**: A hawkish pivot by the Fed or stronger‑than‑expected US data could reverse the dollar’s decline, pressuring gold.
- **Profit‑Taking**: After the sharp rally, short‑term profit‑taking could emerge, especially if diplomatic progress stalls.
- **Mining Cost Inflation**: Persistent wage and energy cost pressures could squeeze miner margins despite higher gold prices.
